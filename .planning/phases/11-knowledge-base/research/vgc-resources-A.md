## 1. Play! Pokemon — Official VGC Rules

**URL:** https://play.pokemon.com/en-us/resources/rules/?category=vgc

### Battle Format

- Double Battles only
- Players bring 6 Pokemon, select 4 to battle with
- All Pokemon levels are set to Level 50 during battle
- Team Preview: 90-second window to view opponent's team before selecting lead 4

### Regulation Sets

VGC uses rotating regulation sets that determine which Pokemon are eligible for competition.
Regulations shift every 3-4 months, synchronized with Ranked Battles in the current main series game.

**Current Regulation: Set F**

Eligible Pokemon:

- Paldea Pokedex numbers 001-398
- Kitakami Pokedex numbers 001-200
- Blueberry Pokedex numbers 001-239, 241, and 242
- Certain other Pokemon explicitly listed as eligible

### Key Mechanics

- **Terastallization**: Each Pokemon may Terastallize once per battle; changes the Pokemon's type
- **Held Items**: All standard held items are permitted (format-specific bans may apply)
- **Abilities**: All standard abilities permitted unless specifically banned

### Age Divisions

- Junior Division
- Senior Division
- Masters Division

### Tournament Structure

- Championship Points awarded by placement at official events
- Events use the current regulation set
- Full official rulebook available as a downloadable PDF from Play! Pokemon

### Notes for AI/Bot Use

- The regulation set determines the legal Pokemon pool — must be parameterized per format
- Level cap of 50 is always enforced regardless of actual Pokemon level
- Team size: always bring 6, always lead with 4 (unless fewer than 4 available)
- Terastallization is a once-per-battle transformation that changes type

## 7. Nugget Bridge — About VGC

**URL:** http://nuggetbridge.com/about-vgc/

### What is VGC?

"VGC stands for Video Game Championships and it's the name of The Pokemon Company International
and Nintendo's official competitive Pokemon tournament circuit."

VGC also refers colloquially to the ruleset itself (formally called "Standard"), which rotates seasonally.

### Tournament Structure Overview

- **Format**: Double Battles
- **Geographic Scope**: US, Canada, Japan, South Korea, Australia, Europe, online via Nintendo Network
- **Qualification Path**: Regional/national events → invite-only Pokemon World Championships
- **World Championships**: "The most prestigious tournament in the game of Pokemon"

### Regional Qualification Pathways

| Region | Method | Spots |
|--------|--------|-------|
| North America | Regional Championships (fall–spring); Championship Points | Top 16 per age division |
| Europe | National Championships (late spring) | Top 32 from Europe/Africa |
| Japan & Korea | Online ladder + national championships; age division finalists qualify | — |
| Australia | Regional tournaments → National Championships at PAX Australia; finalists by age division | — |

### Prizes at World Championships

- Cash scholarships (thousands of dollars for top finishers)
- Exclusive merchandise
- Paid invitations to subsequent World Championships

### Historical Context

- Nugget Bridge was active 2012-2016
- Features articles by legendary VGC players
- Content covers foundational strategy and format history that remains relevant
- Site is archived but not updated; content has historical and educational value

### Notes for AI/Bot Use

- Best source for explaining "what is VGC" to new users in a bot context
- Qualification structure is useful for a tournament-tracking or points-tracking feature
- Historical articles may contain strategy insights that apply across generations
